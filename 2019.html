January 02, 2019

First!

First entry of 2019. I spent December 30th dopamine fasting and chilled on new years eve. I kind of did nothing yesterday but I'm still on my routine. I had a lot of realizations on this fast and ideas about myself particularly. I have all the knowledge now I need to implement them. I feel that 2019 is going to be a good year. 2018 has been extremely life changing. Improve everyday. Currently I am going to resume 12 rules and I'm taking an online course on the science of exercise. I want to go to LindyGroove tomorrow and I've studied up on IP's BeeFriend course. Fasting was good in realizing my demon and internal struggles. It's been cold these days but I've endured.

January 03, 2019

ATP

I've been learning about the science behind exercise this week/today. It has been a good in-depth look at how muscles and energy work, literally. ATP is the reason why our muscles move and is the basis for physical work. A fundamental reaction can build and stack upon itself and eventually even get a man to space, wow. Not much else happening, yesterday we finally beat borderlands 2, op8 and all the raid bosses. I've been feeling kind of demotivated lately and binging zero sum rewards. I think going to LindyGroove tonight will get me out of it. Positive sum rewards right? The cold and the lack of progress is weighing on me. One of the downsides to vacations. Let's read more and watch less. That will be my motto. I'm also going to be more engaging with people to build acquaintances and perhaps friendships. Pretty much do the opposite of last friday. ED: Dad turned off power today to install a new thermostat. I was angry at first but meditated instead to relieve the emotions. Let's get it.

January 06, 2019

Bonsai

I relapsed today. Home alone, lonely, anxious, depressed. Not good circumstances. I'm trying to 180 the day at least be productive with my time for the rest of the day. January 07, 2019

Modern Dance

I woke up pretty early today and worked out. I went to my modern dance class at around noon. It was a bit strange and my first impressions are often really bad reads. Three girls really stood out to me. The class itself was weird to me mostly because of how foreign it was. I've never danced without shoes and socks. I think this might be good for me to get out of my comfort zone and become used to acting weird to a point that it becomes normal. It's all expression after all and there's no wrong way to do that. I was actually expecting the class to be more like jazz or hip hop dancing or like club dancing but it's a lot more like free form dancing with yoga and ballet. There's a lot of stretching and flexibility, range of motion. I'll keep at it since I've committed.

January 12, 2019

Jury Duty

Yesterday I had jury duty. It wasn't bad. My first time at the Pasadena court house. I signed in, went through the basics, read a bit, then went on a two hour lunch break. I walked to Ramen Tatsunoya and had a very rich broth ramen for $19. I chilled at the park on the way back and went back to jury duty. The case itself was really interesting. Something about Carrie Fisher's holdings being disputed by a mother of a heroin user? Apparently it was a pretty big case since there were 113 of us just on Friday and 30ish already coming back Tuesday. I really wanted to find out more about the case since the best lawyers in America were on this case. It also dealt with medicine, drugs, entertainment industry, and some law. But selecting 12ish jurors in a pool of 60-70 people yeah right. I also had dance classes to attend so it was a good way to excuse myself. I'm curious about the case but happy to not do it. After that I walked across the street and read at the Pasadena library. It seemed historic and proper but I was tired. This Saturday morning I woke up late and worked out since I missed it yesterday. It seems that my Twitch habits haven't improved, I spend so much time watching vods or streams. Habit is a bitch. One last note I would like to add is that the experience yesterday showed me that people come from all walks of life and each person is experienced in their own way. To have predispositions and prejudices about anything on an individual is a fault that everyone makes. Even the judge must serve as jury.
